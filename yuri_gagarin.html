Yuri Gagarin

First Human in Space | 1934-1968

Biography

Yuri Gagarin was a Soviet cosmonaut and the first human to travel into space on April 12, 1961. His historic Vostok 1 mission made a single orbit around Earth and proved that spaceflight was possible for humans. He became an international hero and a symbol of Soviet space achievement.

Notable Works

  • First human to orbit Earth on April 12, 1961, aboard Vostok 1.
  • Became an international icon and goodwill ambassador for the Soviet Union.
  • Trained as a pilot and later contributed to Soviet spaceflight training programs.

Awards & Recognitions

  • Hero of the Soviet Union (1961), the highest honor of the USSR.
  • Order of Lenin for his contributions to space exploration.
  • Numerous monuments and places named in his honor, including the Gagarin Cosmonaut Training Center.
